Wholesome Shepherd's Pie

This wholesome, rich shepherd's pie is a fantastic meal for a cold night in fall, or after a long day's work. Sit back with a nice dark beer and a bowl full of this steaming hot pie.

Ingredients

  • One and a half to two pounds of potatoes, peeled and quartered.
  • Eight tablespoons of butter.
  • One medium onion, chopped. (About half a cup.)
  • 1-2 cups of mixed diced peas, carrots and corn.
  • One and a half pounds of ground beef.
  • Half a cup of beef broth.
  • One teaspoon of worcestershire sauce.
  • Salt, pepper, and other seasonings of choice.

    • Procedure

    1. Boil the Potatoes: Place the peeled and quartered potatoes in a medium sized pot. Cover with at least an inch of cold water. Add a teaspoon of salt. Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer, and cook until tender.
    2. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees farenheit.
    3. Saute the vegetables. While the potatoes are cooking, melt 4 tablespoons of butter in a large saute pan on medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until tender, about six to ten minutes. If you are including vegetables, add them according to their cooking time. Carrots should be cooked wiht the onions. Peas or corn should be cooked after the meat starts to cook.
    4. Add the ground beef, worcestershire sauce, and broth. Add the ground beef with the onions and vegetables. Cook until no longer pink, and drain all fat in excess of one tablespoon Season with salt and pepper. Add the sauce and beef broth, then simmer, and reduce to low uncooked for ten minutes. Taste for seasoning.
    5. Mash the potatoes with 4 tablespoons of butter, adding seasoning to taste.
    6. Layer the meat mixture and mashed potatoes in a casserole dish. Spread it in an even, single layer in a large dish. Spread the potatoes on top. Rough up the surface of the potatoes with a fork, so the peaks can get well browned.
    7. Bake at 400 degrees for around 30 minutes, or until browned and bubbling.
